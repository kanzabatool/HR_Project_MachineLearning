Overview¶


In current highly competitive environment, talented people are definitely the most valuable assets. A recent study shows that although hiring has increased in recent years, turnover and attrition rates have also increased for many organizations. This in itself is problematic. However, it's not just the number of employees who leave; it's the types of employees who leave, and reasons they're leaving that deserve some focus. Most of the times, the organization spends for the employee development and grooming and when the same employ resigns it synonymous to companys own backfire as to now, company will suffer from the loss of efforts, time and finances which it pooled in for the very employees development who has now resigned and is all set to practice his skills in some other company.

Keeping this in view, in this project, efforts had been made to find the reason why the best and most experienced of our employees leave prematurely along with predicting which valuable employee will leave the company next. The data worked upon consist of over 11000 employee data collected for various features. Dataset has been explored to view the apparent findings through different visualizing methods. Attribute of employees leaving Vs staying in the company is studied thoroughly. What features are correlated amongst one another are visualized, studied and facts concluded. <br?
Apart from that, departmental analysis is made as to which employees from which department leave the most. Further prediction algorithms are used to classify for the employee falling in which category of whether leaving the company or staying in the company which are our target variables.

Performing the analysis of sample data from typical HR information systems sources, uncovered hidden data patterns to predict present employees in the risk of attrition. The outcome of this study is not only a model predicting with almost 98% accuracy, but is also showing the way how to save a value preventing the possible loss of talents by applying revealed insights.

Features and statistics of the dataset:¶

Advanced analysis has been performed over anonymous corporate employees data. The sample dataset represents prepared and clean data obtained from the HR information systems of a company. The dataset contains common and specific HR-oriented features for utmost 11000 individual employees regarding: satisfaction_level
last_evaluation
number_project
average_monthly_hours
time_spend_company
Work_accident
promotion_last_5years
Department
salary

For each individual record there is an information determining whether the corresponding employee left the company at the end of analyzed period. This information is used to identify key features connected to attrition issue. Uncovering hidden data patterns to predict present employees in the risk of attrition, outcomes of this study show the way how to save a value by identifying possible causes of talents.

Characteristics/abnormalities in the dataset¶

The dataset was processed to check for any missing values in the data however, the data was clean with no missing values so no efforts were required further.

Discussion of algorithms/techniques and Justification¶

KNeighborsClassifier() : We used KNN because it is simple to implement & very straight forward. It is a non-parametric method used for classification and regression. In both cases, the input consists of the k closest training examples in the feature space.
In this problem set, KNeighbor Classifier dealt with a classification problem. An object is classified by a majority vote of its neighbors, with the object being assigned to the class most common among its k nearest neighbors (k is a positive integer, typically small). If k = 1, then the object is simply assigned to the class of that single nearest neighbor.

DecisionTreeClassifier():We used Decision trees as they implicitly perform feature selection & can tackle nonlinear relationships between parameters. Each leaf of the tree is labeled with a class or probability distribution over the classes. A tree can be "learned" by splitting the source set into subsets based on attributes and the recursion is completed when splitting no longer adds value to the predictions. The information gained is based on the decrease in entropy after dataset is split.

RandomForestClassifier() : We tried Random forests as they are an ensemble learning method that operate by constructing multitude of decision trees. Therefore their performance is often better than decision trees alone and can tackle issues like pruning (often an issue in decision tree) automatically.

AdaBoostClassifier() : We also made use of Adaboost as it is used in conjunction with many other types of learning algorithms to improve performance. The output of the other learning algorithms ('weak learners') is combined into a weighted sum that represents the final output of the boosted classifier.

GradientBoostingClassifier() : We used Gradient Boosting Classifier as it produces a prediction model in the form of an ensemble of weak prediction models, typically decision trees. It builds the model in a stage-wise fashion like other boosting methods do, and it generalizes them by allowing optimization of an arbitrary differentiable loss function.

GaussianNB():naive Bayes classifiers are a family of simple probabilistic classifiers based on applying Bayes' theorem with strong (naive) independence assumptions between the features.

LinearDiscriminantAnalysis() : This method was also applied to our problem set, the accuracies werent upto the mark as compared to other classifiers.

It is a method used in statistics, pattern recognition and machine learning to find a linear combination of features that characterizes or separates two or more classes of objects or events. The resulting combination may be used as a linear classifier, or, more commonly, for dimensionality reduction before later classification.

QuadraticDiscriminantAnalysis() : used is a separate measurements of two or more classes of objects or events by a quadric surface. It is a more general version of the linear classifier.

Comparing Accuracies of different classifiers¶

Performance Measured:¶

Following are the metrics that have been used to evaluate the predictions on the classification machine learning problem

(1) Classification accuracy
Classification accuracy is the number of correct predictions made as a ratio of all predictions made. This is the most common evaluation metric for classification problems, it is also the most misused. It is really only suitable when there are an equal number of observations in each class (which is rarely the case) and that all predictions and prediction errors are equally important, which is often not the case.

(2) Logarithmic Loss
Logarithmic loss (or logloss) is a performance metric for evaluating the predictions of probabilities of membership to a given class.

The scalar probability between 0 and 1 can be seen as a measure of confidence for a prediction by an algorithm. Predictions that are correct or incorrect are rewarded or punished proportionally to the confidence of the prediction.

(3) Confusion Matrix
A much better way to evaluate the performance of a classifier is to look at the confusion matrix. The general idea is to count the number of times instances of class A are classified as class B.

(4) Precision:
precision (also called positive predictive value) is the fraction of relevant instances among the retrieved instances.

(5) Recall
Recall (also known as sensitivity) is the fraction of relevant instances that have been retrieved over the total amount of relevant instances.

How the solution could be improved¶

ROC (Receiver Operating Characteristic) can be plotted to see the relative performance of precision and recall of the classifier, as the decision threshold is varied. Thus, when we have different classifiers to choose from, we plot their performances as ROC, and evaluate them. From ROC curve we can also judge the optimum threshold and this way the results can even further be enhanced.

Secondly, a machine learning curve can also be plotted which is useful for many purposes including comparing different algorithms, choosing model parameters during design, adjusting optimization to improve convergence, and determining the amount of data used for training.
